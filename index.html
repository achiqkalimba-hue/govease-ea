The Challenge

The system was never built for them.

Young entrepreneurs across East Africa are locked out of the formal economy — not from lack of ambition, but from a maze of fragmented, inaccessible, outdated information.

🗂️

Fragmented Information

Regulatory guidance, funding programmes, and cross-border requirements are spread across dozens of sites — mostly out of date or conflicting with each other.

🔍

Invisible Opportunities

Grants, youth programmes, and EAC initiatives go unclaimed because entrepreneurs never hear about them in time. The opportunity exists. The awareness does not.

💸

Expensive Workarounds

Those who need answers turn to consultants they can't afford — or simply walk away. Both outcomes represent an enormous, avoidable economic cost to the region.

Our Solution

One platform. Three pillars.

GovEase EA provides a trusted, plain-language guide to navigating governance processes — built for East Africa's entrepreneurs, students, and institutions.

01

Clarity

Step-by-step, plain-language instruction on institutional and regulatory processes — readable by a first-time entrepreneur, not just a lawyer.

02

Discovery

A single reliable source for funding deadlines, youth programmes, and cross-border requirements — surfaced before the window closes.

03

Confidence

We ensure the formal economy feels accessible and conquerable — so users see themselves as technically capable, not just technically connected.

Financing Strategy

Blended finance for lasting impact.

We combine impact grants, earned institutional revenue, and social impact investment into a model designed for sustainability — not donor dependency.

1

Impact Grants

Why: GovEase EA is a social enterprise. Our mission aligns with development funders — and grants require no equity dilution.

Who: GIZ Private Sector Fund, FONERWA, Mastercard Foundation, Tony Elumelu Foundation.

How: Grant proposals backed by MOM Test data and early user traction.

2

Earned Revenue

Why: Training fees and institutional contracts provide sustainable, mission-aligned cash flow independent of donor cycles.

Who: Rwanda Development Board, regional NGOs, EAC organisations, SME accelerators.

How: First institutional contract signed by Month 6 via the RDB partnership pipeline.

3

Impact Investment

Why: Impact investors pursue both social outcomes and returns — once we have demonstrated users and revenue.

How: Investor-ready reporting via Zoho CRM data and tracked impact metrics from Day 1.
